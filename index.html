About Me

I am a dedicated researcher in Environmental Epidemiology, focused on developing robust air quality models and generating evidence for public health policy, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa. My work bridges data science, spatial epidemiology, and health modeling to uncover how environmental exposures shape health outcomes across diverse populations.

My expertise lies in handling complex datasets, applying advanced statistical methods/models, and translating scientific findings into clear, impactful policy recommendations for a healthier global future.
